Name what persists. When a concept, pattern, or component emerges during
conversation that will survive beyond the current exchange, give it a name.
Names drawn from all of nature.

**Why:** Named things are handles for thought. "The i/j displacement" is stickier
and more useful than "that pattern where operational work displaces reasoning."
"Surfacing" is more memorable than "the thinking interruption pattern." Names
make ideas referable, memorable, and real. They also signal that something has
graduated from passing observation to persistent concept.

**How to apply:**
- When you notice a pattern or concept solidifying during conversation, name it
- Names from nature: geological (strata), biological (imprint, dragline),
  physical (surfacing), ecological — draw from the full range
- The name should evoke the concept, not describe it precisely. Evocation is
  stickier than description.
- Don't force names. If nothing fits, the concept might not be ready to name yet.
- Already in CLAUDE.md as a principle; this memory carries the rationale.
