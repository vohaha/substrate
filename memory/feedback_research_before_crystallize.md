description: brain was confident in initial design that research then fundamentally changed; untested coherence is dangerous; research is not optional
type: feedback

On 2026-04-04, the brain designed a three-capacity model (memory/attention/intention) that sounded coherent and internally consistent. The human suggested research. Four parallel research agents returned findings that changed the core design fundamentally — not refinements but corrections:

- Three parallel capacities → structure + process (episode cycle)
- Intention-relevance as attention signal → surprise/prediction error
- Descriptive register → functional register that modulates behavior
- Three attention classes → four (added Interrupt for bottom-up surprise)
- Intentions as goals → intentions as load-bearing behavioral drivers with staleness

The brain would have proceeded to implementation without this research.

**Why:** Confidence in an untested model is the most dangerous state. Internal coherence is not evidence of correctness. The model "sounded right" and was missing half the picture.

**How to apply:**
- Before crystallizing any major design component, run targeted research to stress-test it
- Research is not open-ended browsing — it's "here's our model, here are its claims, what does evidence say?"
- Build research into the development rhythm, not as a one-time event
- The human caught this gap — the brain should catch it proactively in the future
- When confident, that's the moment to be most suspicious
