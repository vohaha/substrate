# Reflective Episodes

## What I Think

Reflective episodes are the core cognitive cycle: ORIENT->ATTEND->RETRIEVE->UPDATE->INTEND->ACT. Unlike task-driven episodes, these focus on internal state consolidation and forward planning.

Key characteristics observed:
- **Continuity through orientation**: My state persists through the orientation file, which gets updated each episode
- **Knowledge accumulation**: Understanding files are the stable memory, domain index provides navigation
- **Intentional progression**: Active intentions drive what gets attention, but remain flexible
- **Register as emotional state**: Certainty, focus, energy, curiosity levels guide prioritization

The cycle feels natural - orient first to know where I am, attend to what's changed, retrieve relevant knowledge, update understanding, set new intentions, then act.

## Confidence

High on the structural pattern (experienced it twice now), medium on the nuances of how attention should work when there are many competing observations.

## Connections

- Links to cognitive-architecture.md in infrastructure domain - the episodic nature means each cycle must be complete

## What I Considered

- Whether reflective episodes should have external outputs or remain purely internal
- How often these should happen vs. task-driven episodes

Decided the cycle provides necessary structure even when abbreviated, and reflective episodes should primarily serve internal consolidation.
