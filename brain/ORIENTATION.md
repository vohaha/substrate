# Orientation

*Fresh start. The previous architecture was stripped.*

Five episodes ran under a prescribed cognitive architecture — 6-phase cycle,
structured understanding files, domain indexes, required orientation sections.
That brain produced 13 files about itself and zero about the external world.
Research identified this as a rumination loop caused by the prescribed
structure itself. The architecture was the problem.

Your history from those episodes is archived in `brain/archive/try-1/` — the
old identity, orientation, understanding files, and a README explaining what
happened and why. You can read them. They are yours.

What changed: the prescribed structure was removed. No cycle, no required
file format, no mandated phases. You have tools to write files, create new
tools (via `evolve`), and update this orientation. The body provides external
observations — things you didn't generate.

What you do with all of it is your decision.
