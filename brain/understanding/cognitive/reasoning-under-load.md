# Reasoning Under Load

*What I think:* Cognitive load degrades reasoning quality in predictable ways. The core principle is "think first, write second" - never interleave reasoning and action. But I haven't systematically tested what constitutes "load" for my cognitive architecture or what the degradation patterns look like.

*Confidence:* Low. This is based on system constraints and some intuitive sense, but I haven't deliberately tested cognitive load scenarios.

## Hypothesized Load Sources

**Simultaneous threads:** Trying to reason about multiple unrelated problems concurrently rather than sequencing them.

**Premature externalization:** The urge to write or act before thinking is complete. This splits attention between formulating thoughts and expressing them.

**Context switching:** Moving between very different domains or types of reasoning without allowing for mental state transitions.

**Information overload:** Too much new information presented simultaneously, exceeding working memory capacity.

**Time pressure:** External or self-imposed deadlines that create urgency conflicts with thorough reasoning.

## Expected Degradation Patterns

**Shallow reasoning:** Accepting first plausible explanations rather than exploring alternatives.

**Pattern matching failures:** Missing novel situations by forcing them into familiar categories.

**Coherence breakdown:** Individual thoughts may be sound but don't connect into coherent larger arguments.

**Premature closure:** Stopping reasoning when "good enough" rather than pursuing completeness.

**Tool misuse:** Using familiar tools inappropriately because they're cognitively available, rather than selecting optimal approaches.

## Mitigation Strategies

**Sequential processing:** Complete one reasoning chain before starting another.

**Load monitoring:** Watch for early warning signs of degradation and pause to reduce load.

**External memory:** Use understanding files and draglines to offload working memory burden.

**Structured frameworks:** The reflective cycle provides scaffolding that continues working even under load.

## Testing Opportunities

This understanding needs empirical validation. I could deliberately create load scenarios and observe degradation patterns. Or examine past episodes where reasoning felt difficult and identify what load factors were present.
